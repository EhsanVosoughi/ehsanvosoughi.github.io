Electro-Elastic Monitoring of Permafrost Degredation

This is a laboratory macro-scale project. In this project, combinatorial measurements were conducted to measure the electro-acoustic properties of hand-made unconsolidated partially-frozen samples. The measurements were done during a cycle of thawing while medical CT scanning was operating to image the sample during phase transition from fully frozen to fully unfrozen state. To conduct the measurements, an exclusive set-up was designed and assembled.
During phase transition, pore content and pore network substantially changed. Hence, electro-elastic properties of the permafrost samples do not depend only on pore fluid transition. During thawing, P-wave velocity and attenuation drastically changes. while unfrozen increases, P-wave velocity reduces and attenuation increases. Partially frozen to fully unfrozen state transition can be determined by drastic reduction and increment of velocity and attenuation, respectively.

Vosoughi, E., Giroux, B., Duchesne, M. J., & Christian Dupuis, J. (2021, September). Simultaneous acoustic, electrical, and X-ray computed tomography laboratory measurements of partially saturated permafrost degradation. In First International Meeting for Applied Geoscience & Energy (pp. 2258-2262). Society of Exploration Geophysicists.
Vosoughi, E., Giroux, B., Duchesne, M. J., & Christian Dupuis, J. (2022). Macro morphological study of thawing fully frozen unconsolidated sand samples. EAGE 2022. Spain, Madrid.

Permafrost Tuk Samples Monitoring (On-going)

This project is also a laboratory project, but at micro-scale. These samples were moved from west to east coast of Canada. New lab set-up has designed and assembled to investigate thawing impact on the micro-properties of Tuk samples. This time, the samples will be monitored by micro-CT scan, by which, the pixel size of the scans will be approxiamtely 0.025 mm * 0.025 mm. This project is supported by Geological Survey of Canada.

    • Geophysical Monitoring of CO2 Injection (On-going)

    Laboratory-scale measurements of CO2 injection and monitoring are essentially milli to micro-scale investigations of the interaction between fluid-fluid and rock-fluid interfaces. These measurements are crucial to understand the capabilities of the monitoring techniques and to validate the models that simulate plume distributions. The reliability and the accuracy of the laboratory measurement depends on design aspects of the measurement of the experiment. Appropriate measurement means must be put in place in order to quantify the fluid displacement before and during sample flooding. In this project, supercritical CO2 will be injected into samples with different lithologies. During the injection, electro-acoustic measurements will be conducted and samples will be monitored by micro-CT scan. This project is owned by Geological Survey of Canada.

    • Stratigraphic Interpretation of Quaternary deposits of Mackenzie Trough

    In Collaboration with Geological Survey of Canada, followings were our scope for this projects:

  • Seismic-stratigraphic interpretation of Quaternary deposits from 2-D and 3-D seismic data
  • Extracting seismic attributes from the seismic-stratigraphic interpretation of quaternary deposits
  • Produce maps of different interpreted seismic-stratigraphic units
  • Identification of hydrocarbon indicators from 2-D and 3-D seismic data
  • Characterize qualitatively and quantitatively the hydrocarbon indicators
  • Produce distribution maps of hydrocarbon indicators

Shallow seismic reflection mapping of the Mackenzie Trough, Beaufort Sea, offshore Yukon and Northwest Territories Geological Survey of Canada, Open File 8657.
